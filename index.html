I am currently a Ph.D. candidate in Chemical Engineering at University of California San Diego completeing my research at Scripps Institution of Oceanography.

I use machine learning to study air-ice-ocean interactions. My current work is aimed at using machine learning to understand how the dynamical properties of sea ice in the Arctic are changing as the ice melts. I employ explainable AI (XAI) techniques to understand changes in the drivers of variability of ice motion, with particular focus given to the changing relationship between wind and ice speed.
