I am currently a postdoctoral researcher at Université Catholique de Louvain in Louvain-la-Neuve, Belgium.

I use machine learning techniques to study sea ice in the Arctic. My current work is aimed at using machine learning to predict and understand rapid sea-ice loss. I also employ explainable AI (XAI) techniques to understand changes in the drivers of variability of ice motion, with particular focus given to the changing relationship between wind and ice speed.
